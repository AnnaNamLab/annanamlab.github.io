Sara Moein, PhD - Research Associate

Sara is experienced in developing and applying machine learning and optimization algorithms on genetics and healthcare data for knowledge discovery. Predicting the outcome of healthcare procedures, modeling gene behavior, and discovering associations between genetic defects and diseases are among her research interests.

Mirca Saras Saurty-Seerunghen, PhD - Post-doctoral fellow

Mirca’s PhD work focused on single-cell transcriptomics data analysis and experimental validation for discovery of targetable metabolism enzymes in cancer. She is interested in uncovering the cell fate decisions in hematopoietic neoplasms by linking cell state and genetic lineage histories in single cells.

Minwoo Pak, PhD - Post-doctoral fellow

Minwoo received his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science & Engineering, focusing on the development of machine learning and deep learning methods for analyzing multi-omics data. He hopes to further enhance his expertise in analyzing single-cell multi-omics data, aiming to build a strong foundation in this area. He has a strong passion for embracing new and innovative approaches, and he enjoys the process of working with new data and growing through it.

Mathis Nozais, PhD - Post-doctoral fellow

Mathis pursued his PhD in genomics and data analysis, where he applied single-cell transcriptomics to gain insights into leukemogenesis. He aims to further expand his expertise by integrating multi-omics approaches, with the goal of better understanding cellular heterogeneity and cancer mechanisms. When not in the lab he loves cooking and running to explore the city.

Ana Spajic, MD - Postdoctoral Fellow

Ana is currently an Anatomic Pathology resident at NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital/Weill Cornell Medicine. She will be heading to Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in 2027-2028 to complete a fellowship in dermatopathology. Ana was recently awarded the Tri-I StARR Award, which will enable her to spend her final year of residency investigating the genetics of acral melanoma. Outside of work, Ana enjoys playing tennis, running, and exploring New York City’s food scene.

Ted Kang, BA - Computational Research Assistant

Ted is a graduate from Princeton University, where he earned his bachelor's degree in Computer Science. He is excited to gain research experience and learn more about immunology before eventually pursuing a graduate degree in Computational Biology. Outside of work, he enjoys playing basketball, cooking, and watching movies.

Leila Zaydon - Volunteer Research Assistant

Leila is a current undergraduate student at Barnard College, majoring in Biology on the pre-medical track. She is excited to explore her interests in molecular biology and hematopoiesis while in the lab. She plans to pursue an MD degree after graduation. Outside of the lab, she enjoys running, traveling, and spending time with family.
