Understanding genotype-to-phenotype interplay in cancer development.

The Anna Nam Lab studies how somatic mutations, cellular state, and tissue context shape neoplastic development and clinical outcomes.

Why

Somatic mutations drive cancer, yet patients with the same mutations can develop distinct disease states and outcomes. We seek to understand why.

How

We develop single-cell multi-omics technologies and computational methods to connect genotype, epigenetic state, transcriptome, and phenotype.

Who

We are building a diverse, collaborative community of compassionate scientists with a strong desire to make fundamental discoveries for translational impact.

Research Direction

Deciphering cancer evolution one cell at a time.

The lab integrates molecular biology, hematopathology, single-cell assays, and computation to study how genetic and non-genetic determinants shape malignant transformation.
